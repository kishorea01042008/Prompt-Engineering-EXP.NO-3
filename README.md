Procedure:

Example:

Straightforward Prompts:

• "Define photosynthesis in one sentence."

"List three advantages of electric vehicles."

Tabular Format Prompting:

• "Compare and contrast AC and DC current in a table."

• "Provide a table listing five programming languages, their paradigms, and one use case each."

Preceding Question Prompting:

• "Why is climate change a global concern? Explain how greenhouse gases contribute to global warming."

• "How do vaccines work? Describe the process of immunization in simple terms."

Missing Word Prompting:

• "The capital of France is ____."

• "In photosynthesis, plants absorb sunlight to produce ____."

OUTPUT:

1. Straightforward Prompts
These are direct, clear instructions that ask the AI to perform a specific task without additional formatting constraints or conversational lead-ins.

Scenario A: Biomimicry in Architecture

Prompt: "Explain how the structural design of the Eiffel Tower was influenced by the anatomy of the human femur bone."

Scenario B: Culinary Chemistry

Prompt: "Describe the chemical process of 'spherification' used in molecular gastronomy to create balsamic vinegar pearls."

2. Tabular Format Prompting
This technique asks the AI to organize complex data into a structured grid, making it easier to compare variables at a glance.

Scenario A: Comparing Ancient Megastructures

Prompt: "Create a table comparing the Great Pyramid of Giza, the Parthenon, and Teotihuacan. Include columns for: Primary Material, Estimated Labor Force, Purpose, and Unique Engineering Feature."

Scenario B: Exoplanet Habitability

Prompt: "Generate a table for three known exoplanets (e.g., Kepler-186f, Proxima Centauri b, TRAPPIST-1e). Include columns for: Distance from Earth, Star Type, Estimated Surface Temperature, and Key Barrier to Human Colonization."

3. Preceding Question Prompting
This method uses an initial question to establish context or "prime" the AI’s reasoning before asking for the final output. It helps ensure the model is thinking in the right domain.

Scenario A: Deep-Sea Exploration Ethics

Prompt: "Is the deep ocean floor considered common heritage of mankind? Based on the answer, draft a proposal for a sustainable deep-sea mining regulation that protects hydrothermal vent ecosystems."

Scenario B: Cybernetic Integration

Prompt: "What are the current limitations of Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCIs) regarding signal latency? Given these limitations, write a sci-fi micro-story about a professional gamer whose career depends on a prototype neural link."

4. Missing Word Prompting
Also known as "Fill-in-the-blank" or Cloze prompting, this technique forces the AI to provide specific terminology or concepts within a pre-defined sentence structure.

Scenario A: Astrophysics Principles

Prompt: "In the lifecycle of a massive star, when the core can no longer support itself against gravity, it undergoes a ____ collapse, resulting in either a ____ or a ____."

Scenario B: Historical Linguistics

Prompt: "The 'Great Vowel Shift' was a major change in the ____ of the English language that took place primarily between 1400 and 1700, causing a distinct separation between ____ and ____."
